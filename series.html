The Office

Director: Ken Kwapis, Paul Feig, Randall Einhorn, and Greg Daniels

Review:I've watched this entire show and at first, I was bored and didn't want to continue watching, but once I got closer to the second season I was hooked. I started to understand the characters' personalities better which helped me understand the show better. This show is funny, sweet, and overall very good. The Office shows a lot of romance, comedy, and realistic fiction all at the same time. The humor is dry humor. Some people aren't a fan of it, but I found it hilarious the longer I watched and the more development the characters had.

Breaking Bad

Director: Vince Gilligan

Review:"Breaking Bad" is a masterpiece that seamlessly blends intense drama, brilliant storytelling, and exceptional character development. Vince Gilligan's creation is a gripping exploration of the transformation of Walter White from a mild-mannered chemistry teacher to a formidable drug lord. The writing is nothing short of brilliant, keeping viewers on the edge of their seats with each episode.

Game Of Thrones

Director: David Nutter

Review: First things first, it's very geeky. It's got kings and knights, crowns and swords, dragons and ghosts. Medieval drama is a genre we get precious little of on television (or in movies, for that matter), and Game of Thrones has nearly 10 hours of it. Plus, the series of books boasts fans who are as serious about the happenings in Westeros – where most of the book's action takes place – as any hardcore Trekkie or Star Wars geek is about those sagas.

Friends

Director: David Crane and Marta Kauffman

Review: Friends is one of the greatest and best sitcoms of all time. Friends is about the lives of six unique adults who overcome different adventures and experiences which makes their lives both troublesome and entertaining. Jennifer Aniston portrays Rachel Green, Courteney Cox portrays Monica Geller, Lisa Kudrow portrays Phoebe Buffay, David Schwimmer portrays Ross Geller, Matt Leblanc portrays Joey Tribbiani, and Mathew Perry portrays Chandler Bing.

Brooklyn Nine-Nine

Director: Linda Mendoza

Review: Funny, hilarious, heartbreaking and serious at the same time, Brooklyn Nine-Nine is a masterful comedy infused with serious discussion about current affairs while having powerful and lovable characters that will stay with you long after you finish the episode or season.

Mindhunter

Director: David Fincher

Review: Mindhunter is an absolutely riveting and intellectually gripping series that redefines the crime drama genre. From the very first episode, it plunges you into a hauntingly realistic portrayal of the early days of criminal profiling at the FBI. The attention to detail, the slow-burn narrative, and the chillingly accurate depictions of real-life serial killers make it one of the most intense and thought-provoking shows I’ve ever watched.
